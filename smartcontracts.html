What is a Smart Contract?

A smart contract is a self-executing agreement where the terms are written into code and executed automatically when certain conditions are met. It's like a digital agreement that runs without needing a middleman or lawyer to enforce it. Smart contracts are deployed on a blockchain network such as Ethereum or Polygon.

How Does It Work?

  • 1. Create the contract: You define the terms and conditions of the contract (e.g., when to transfer funds, trigger a payment, etc.).
  • 2. Deploy the contract: We deploy the contract to a blockchain like Ethereum or Polygon for automation.
  • 3. Execute Automatically: Once the conditions are met, the smart contract executes actions automatically (like transferring funds, sending tokens, etc.).

What Do You Need to Use Smart Contracts?

To use smart contracts, you'll need the following:

  • A Blockchain Wallet: Both parties need a wallet like MetaMask or Trust Wallet.
  • Ethereum or Polygon for Gas Fees: You'll need cryptocurrency (ETH for Ethereum or MATIC for Polygon) to pay transaction fees for deploying and interacting with the contract.
  • Contract Terms: Define what you want the contract to do—e.g., trigger a payment when certain conditions are met, transfer tokens, etc.
  • Internet Connection: A stable connection to interact with the blockchain network.
