Web Design Checklist for Solo Practitioners & Boutique Law Firms

1. Define Your Website’s Purpose

  • Set clear objectives: What do you want your website to achieve? (e.g., lead generation, consultations, case information, etc.)
  • Identify target audience: Who are you focusing on (e.g., individuals needing legal help, small business owners)?
  • Decide on your brand personality: What kind of professional image do you want to project (approachable, authoritative, innovative)?

2. Choose a Simple & Memorable Domain Name

  • Pick a short, memorable domain: E.g., YourNameLaw.com or CityLawyer.com
  • Consider a location-based domain: E.g., “DallasDivorceAttorney.com” if focusing on a specific region.
  • Avoid numbers and hyphens: Choose a clean, easy-to-remember name.

3. Mobile-Friendly & Easy Navigation

  • Ensure mobile optimization: Does your website adjust seamlessly to mobile and tablet screens?
  • Keep navigation simple: Limit your menu to essential pages (e.g., Home, About, Practice Areas, Testimonials, Contact).
  • Use visible CTAs: Ensure calls-to-action like “Request a Consultation” or “Contact Us” are visible on all pages.

4. Personalize Your Branding

  • Create a professional logo: Is your logo clear and professional, reflecting your practice's values?
  • Choose a consistent color palette: Stick to 2-3 colors that align with the legal profession (e.g., navy, dark gray, white).

5. Highlight Your Services Clearly

  • List your practice areas: Provide clear and concise descriptions of the services you offer.
  • Simplify service descriptions: Keep each description short and client-friendly, avoiding legal jargon.
  • Focus on your top areas: Showcase the specific legal services you excel at, especially if they differentiate your firm.
